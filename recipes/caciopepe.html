Cacio Pepe

Description

A famous chef once said "All you need in life is good espresso, sweet gelato and cacio e pepe". Cacio e Pepe is a simple dish and the specialty of the capital of Italy, Rome. The dish was originally peasant food - the simple ingredients certainly allude to that.

Ingredients

  • 350g of spaghetti
  • 220g of pecorino romano parmesan cheese
  • 14g of whole black peppercorns
  • 1,750 liters of water
  • 1/2 tablespoon of coarse salt

Steps:

  1. Start by grating the pecorino romano cheese. When ready, set aside in a bowl.
  2. Grind the black peppercorns. You can use a pestle for this, a meat pounder or anything you have at hand. The black pepper should not be a powder.
  3. Toast the ground pepper in a large skillet over medium heat. Stir occasionally and check to make sure it doesn't burn. It should take around 2-3 minutes, then set aside.
  4. Now boil the water with very coarse salt to cook the pasta. Let the pasta cook for about 5 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, add 2 ladles of pasta water to the toasted pepper and turn the heat to low.
  6. After the 5 minutes, transfer the pasta to the skillet. The pasta will finish cooking here with the pepper.
  7. Add another ladle of water if needed and stir to get even cooking.
  8. While the pasta cooks, make the pecorino cream. Pour a ladle of the starchy pasta water over the grated cheese and stir quickly until you get a ricotta like consistency.
  9. Once the spaghetti is cooked, turn off the heat, wait about half a minute and add the pecorino cream a little at a time and you're done!
